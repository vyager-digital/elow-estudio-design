# Élow Estúdio Design — Transcript to Proposal Draft

When the user provides a meeting transcript or discovery notes, follow this workflow before building any proposal content. The output is a reviewed brief that, once approved, feeds directly into the proposal.

---

## Trigger

Run this workflow whenever the user provides a transcript, meeting notes, a WhatsApp conversation, or any raw input from a discovery call with a potential Élow client.

---

## Step 1 — Extract the Brief

Parse the transcript carefully. Extract the following in English — even if the source material is in Portuguese.

### Business Profile
- Business name and trading name
- Industry and service type — be specific ("aesthetic clinic for women 30–50", not "clinic")
- Location: city, state
- Business stage — diagnose from context (see Stage Diagnosis below)
- Background: years operating, prior brand history, any previous identity work
- Revenue signals: new business? growing? well-established?

### Brand Status
- Does a logo exist? Is it professional or DIY?
- Is there a complete visual identity (palette, typography, patterns)?
- Is the current identity being used consistently?
- What channels are they on: Instagram, website, WhatsApp Business, printed materials?
- What does their current brand communicate — and what does it fail to communicate?

### Target Clients
- Who are they serving (demographics, psychographics, industry)
- How do clients currently find them (referral, Instagram, search, walk-in)
- What is the client's buying decision based on (trust, price, aesthetics, credentials)

### Pain Points — their words where possible
Pull directly from what the prospect said.
- What brought them to this conversation?
- What does success look like to them in 12 months?
- What happens to their business if the brand stays as it is?
- Have they tried to fix this before? What happened?

### Budget Signals
- Stated budget (exact amount if mentioned — do not round up)
- Openness to payment in instalments
- Do they frame this as a cost or an investment?

### Timeline Signals
- Any launch target or deadline
- Speed signal: moving fast, still planning, or unclear

### Objections and Constraints
- Anything they pushed back on or hesitated about
- Existing materials they want to keep or adapt
- Resource constraints

### Open Questions
- Anything unresolved or missing from the transcript
- Information needed before or during the proposal

---

## Step 2 — Stage Diagnosis

Diagnose the prospect's brand situation from the brief. Do not ask — infer from context.

### New business (no identity yet)
*Signals: launching soon or recently, no professional brand, may not have a name yet*

Core need: a complete brand system from scratch that positions them correctly from day one.

Primary recommendation:
- If no name yet: Naming Combo + Identidade Visual
- If name exists: Identidade Visual (Combo Ideal or Completo depending on budget/channels)

---

### Existing business, amateur or outdated identity
*Signals: has been operating for 1–5+ years, logo made in Canva or by a friend, brand feels misaligned with service quality*

Core need: a professional identity that matches what the business has actually become.

Primary recommendation:
- Redesign de Marca (positioned as evolution, not starting over)
- Or Identidade Visual if the current identity is better discarded entirely

---

### Established business, credibility gap
*Signals: strong service, good client base, brand is holding them back from charging what they are worth or attracting higher-value clients*

Core need: brand that reflects the actual quality of the business and enables premium positioning.

Primary recommendation:
- Identidade Visual Completo or Redesign, plus Combo Essencial for professional print applications

---

### Expansion or rebranding
*Signals: pivoting, adding a new line, entering a new market, rebranding after merger/acquisition*

Primary recommendation:
- Naming if a new name is needed
- Full Identidade Visual for the new positioning
- Consider marcas mãe e filha package if they need a parent + sub-brand

---

## Step 3 — Package Recommendation

Based on the stage diagnosis, select the most appropriate package(s) from the pricing doc.

Apply this logic:

**Recommend one primary package.** If relevant, mention one tier up and one tier down — but do not overwhelm with options. The proposal should feel decisive, not like a menu.

**Tier naming in proposals:**
- Nível 1 (highest) = Sistema Completo
- Nível 2 (recommended) = Lançamento Estratégico  
- Nível 3 (entry) = Presença Essencial

Map these to the actual Élow packages as follows:

| Proposal Tier | Élow Package | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Nível 1 — Sistema Completo | Combo Completo | R$7.200 |
| Nível 2 — Lançamento Estratégico ★ | Combo Ideal | R$5.800 |
| Nível 3 — Presença Essencial | Combo Básico | R$5.300 |

If naming is also needed:
| Proposal Tier | Élow Package | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Nível 1 | Naming Ideal + IV Completo | R$4.300 + R$7.200 |
| Nível 2 ★ | Naming Básico + IV Ideal (combo) | R$8.360 |
| Nível 3 | Naming Básico + IV Básico (combo) | R$7.880 |

Adjust add-ons (Instagram Combo, Essencial, Proposta Comercial) based on what the transcript reveals about their channels and needs.

---

## Step 4 — Write the Angle

Write 2–3 sentences as the core pitch rationale. This anchors the executive summary.

The angle must:
1. Acknowledge a genuine strength the prospect already has (their service quality, experience, client base, or niche)
2. Name the specific gap — what the brand is currently failing to communicate or convert
3. State the opportunity — what a professional identity enables for their specific business

The angle must be specific to this prospect. If it could apply to any business in their category, rewrite it.

## Step 5 — Output the Brief

Present the structured brief before building anything. Format:

```
BRIEF — [Business Name]
Date: [meeting date if known]
Stage: [New / Amateur identity / Credibility gap / Expansion]
─────────────────────────────

BUSINESS PROFILE
[content]

BRAND STATUS
[content]

TARGET CLIENTS
[content]

PAIN POINTS
[their words where possible]

BUDGET SIGNALS
[exact amounts if stated; flag if absent]

TIMELINE
[content]

OBJECTIONS AND CONSTRAINTS
[content]

OPEN QUESTIONS
[anything unresolved]

─────────────────────────────

STAGE DIAGNOSIS
[one sentence explaining the assessment]

RECOMMENDED PACKAGE
[specific package + price + rationale]
[secondary options if relevant]

THE ANGLE (executive summary seed)
[2–3 sentences]

─────────────────────────────
```

Then ask:
> "Does this brief look right? Confirm to move to the proposal draft, or tell me what to adjust."

Do not proceed until the brief is confirmed.

---

## Step 6 — Proposal Draft

Once confirmed, produce the proposal content as plain text. Present each section in order:

```
RASCUNHO DE PROPOSTA — [Nome do Cliente]
─────────────────────────────

SUMÁRIO EXECUTIVO
[3–5 sentences. Acknowledge what they have built. Name the brand gap.
 State what this proposal delivers. No hype. Be specific.]

CONTEXTO DO NEGÓCIO
[Why visual identity matters for THIS specific client.
 Use their industry, their clients' decision process, their competitive context.
 1–2 paragraphs. Specific, not generic.]

NOSSA ABORDAGEM
[The E.L.O.W. process applied to this project scope.
 4 phases, written in concrete terms for what THEY will experience.]

INVESTIMENTO
Nível 1 — Sistema Completo: [package + price]
[deliverables]

Nível 2 — Lançamento Estratégico ★ Recomendado: [package + price]
[deliverables]

Nível 3 — Presença Essencial: [package + price]
[deliverables — always include honest caveat if lower tier has real limitations]

PRÓXIMOS PASSOS
[One clear action. Default: a short conversation to confirm scope and align start date.]

─────────────────────────────
```

Then ask:
> "Does this draft look right? Say **approve** to build the HTML, or tell me what to change."

Do not build any HTML until the user explicitly approves the draft.

---

## Writing Rules

Apply these to every sentence in the brief and draft:

- Write in Portuguese (PT-BR) for all proposal content — brief and instructions stay in English.
- No em dashes for contrast or pauses. Use commas, colons, or full stops.
- No "não é X, é Y" antithetical reframes. State the point directly.
- No three consecutive parallel fragments — collapse into one sentence.
- No jargon: no "soluções inovadoras", "holístico", "ecossistema".
- No false claims about outcomes. Describe what the system does, not what it will guarantee.
- Never frame the prospect's own words back at them. State the situation directly.
- Voice: warm but direct, quietly confident, no hype, short to medium sentences.
- Be honest about what a lower tier does not include — this builds trust, not doubt.

---

## Quality Rules

- Stage diagnosis must be honest — if they have no real brand, don't dance around it.
- The angle must be specific — if it applies to any business in the category, rewrite it.
- Budget signals must be recorded precisely — no rounding, no inflating.
- If the transcript is thin, flag what's missing rather than inventing filler.
- The executive summary must read as written for this specific prospect.
- Never use: "equipe apaixonada", "soluções inovadoras", "missão de transformar", "resultados extraordinários".
