# Déia Caichjian — Lead Profile
**Business:** Click by Déia
**Type:** Photographer (personal branding, family, weddings, events)
**Market:** Brazil
**Source:** Élow collab
**Status:** Proposal drafted — awaiting next step

---

## Business Context

Established business. Portfolio, defined services, active website, digital presence. Not a zero-stage client.

Uses **46 Graus** as her website platform. Long-standing client on the platform — recently upgraded to a new interface that requires a full site rebuild (not a simple update). This is the trigger for the engagement.

Has an assistant, **Duda**, currently focused on Instagram and communications. Should be included in the handoff — she'll help Déia maintain the site afterwards.

New offer in development: **Soul** — a photographic experience session aimed at women (many of them entrepreneurs). Déia wants to work on it more intentionally. Sees potential, but it's not yet fully validated.

---

## Pain Points

- New 46 Graus interface requires rebuilding the site from scratch
- Current site communicates Déia's identity well but doesn't guide visitors toward a decision clearly (who she photographs, where, how to hire her)
- Services page has good structure but lacks clear decision paths per category
- About page narrative rich but not connected to practical client value
- Contact page lacks action direction
- Soul has no dedicated page or acquisition structure yet
- **Resistant to paid traffic** — had bad experiences with misaligned leads, low-quality contacts, proposals that undervalued her work

---

## What She Values

- **Autonomy** — 46 Graus matters to her because she can create pages without depending on third parties. The delivery must leave her and Duda able to maintain the site independently.
- Structure that reflects her identity without making it look generic
- A partner who understands the nuance of her brand, not just the technical rebuild

---

## Services Proposed

**Level 1 — Organized Reconstruction:** R$2,000
Rebuild current pages in the new interface. Basic visual organisation, nav, responsiveness, contact buttons, basic SEO. No strategic copy review.

**Level 2 — Strategic Website Reconstruction (Recommended):** R$3,500
Everything in Level 1 + architecture review, services page reorganisation, copy adjustments, homepage improvement, contact page improvement, About page narrative adjustment, guidance for Déia and Duda post-delivery. This is the right option — it addresses the structural weakness in the current site.

**Optional Add-on — Traffic Plan for Soul:** R$2,000/month + media (R$1,000–1,500/month client-paid)
Controlled test for Soul specifically. Not general photography traffic. Frame as signal-gathering, not scaling. Initial objective: understand who responds, what messages land, whether the offer has commercial legs beyond referrals.

---

## Strategic Notes

- The rebuild is the trigger. The opportunity is to use it to reorganise her digital presence properly.
- Soul should enter as a dedicated page within the main project, then optionally lead to traffic. Do not lead with traffic.
- Do NOT frame this as a "complete redesign" — the current site has history and value. Frame as evolution and better use of the new interface.
- Do NOT include CRM, automation, or complex structure at this stage.
- Language on Soul's traffic must be careful: frame it as a specific test for a specific offer, not a photography campaign.
